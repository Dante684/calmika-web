For children on the autism spectrum, predictability is not a luxury — it's a necessity. A visual schedule is a simple yet highly effective tool that helps reduce anxiety and difficulties around transitions.

## Why Do We Need Visual Schedules?

Many ASD children have **strong visual processing abilities**. They understand what they see more easily than what they hear. Visualizing the schedule acts as a "translation" for the child.

### Managing Transitions

One of the biggest challenges for ASD children is **managing transitions** — switching from one activity to another. A visual schedule shows in advance what comes next, so the switch won't be a surprise.

## How to Build a Visual Schedule

### Step 1: Choose Your Format

- **Paper-based:** Printed pictures attached with velcro
- **Board-based:** Whiteboard or magnetic board with picture cards
- **Digital:** Tablet app (e.g., Calmika)

### Step 2: Define the Day's Structure

Start with the main milestones:
- Wake up
- Breakfast
- School / therapy
- Lunch
- Free time
- Dinner
- Evening routine
- Sleep

### Step 3: Add Details Gradually

Don't start with the whole day at once! Begin by visualizing just the morning routine, then expand.
